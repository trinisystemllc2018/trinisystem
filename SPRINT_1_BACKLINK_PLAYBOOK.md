# Sprint 1 — Backlink Playbook for Google Sites Blog

This document gives you the **exact anchor text and link snippets** to paste into your existing Google Sites blog posts. The goal: pass topical relevance and PageRank from your blog → main domain.

Your existing blog: https://sites.google.com/view/trini-system-llc-garmin-nuvi/Garmingpsmapupdate

## Why this matters

Google Sites pages rank decently for long-tail queries, but they're capped — Google won't let a Sites page outrank your main domain on commercial keywords. Use the Sites blog as a **link asset**: each blog page should send 2-4 contextual links to the relevant page on `trinisystem.vercel.app`.

This is not link spam. It's standard internal linking across two properties you own.

## Anchor text guidelines

When writing or editing your Google Sites pages, vary the anchor text. Don't link the same phrase 5 times — Google penalizes "exact match anchor over-optimization."

✅ **Good variety:**
- "Garmin DriveSmart update guide"
- "DriveSmart 65 troubleshooting"
- "see our DriveSmart help"
- "we've covered this in detail here"

❌ **Bad (over-optimized):**
- 5 different links all using "garmin drivesmart update" as anchor

---

## Frequency rule

**Maximum 4 outbound links per blog post** to your main domain. More than that and Google's algorithms can flag the page as a thin-content link farm.

Distribution:
- 1 link in the intro / above the fold
- 2-3 contextual links inside body content (where they genuinely help the reader)
- 1 in a closing "more help" section

---

## What to do AFTER adding these links

1. **Wait 48 hours** for Google to re-crawl your Sites pages
2. **Submit the new main-domain pages to Google Search Console** for indexing
3. **Check Bing Webmaster Tools** for the new URLs as well
4. **In 2 weeks, check Google Analytics on the new pages** to see if Sites referral traffic appears
